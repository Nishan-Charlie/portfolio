About

I am an Honors Engineering graduate specialised in Computer Engineering from the University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka. Currently, I serve as a Lecturer (Probationary) at the University of Sabaragamuwa, where I am also the Assistant Research Coordinator.

My work sits at the intersection of Computer Vision, 3D Reconstruction, Hybrid Quantum-Classical Machine Learning, and Explainable AI (XAI). I am particularly interested in building robust, interpretable models for agricultural and medical image analysis, and in exploring how 3D computer vision and quantum computing can augment classical deep learning pipelines.

Beyond research, I am passionate about bridging rigorous academic methodology with practical deployment — from federated learning on edge devices to semi-supervised learning under label scarcity.

Research Interests

  • Computer Vision & 3D Reconstruction — 3D computer vision, SLAM, point cloud analysis, Vision Transformers, object detection, semantic segmentation, self-supervised & semi-supervised learning
  • Quantum Machine Learning — Hybrid quantum-classical architectures, variational quantum circuits, quantum feature maps
  • Explainable AI (XAI) — Attention visualisation, Grad-CAM, LIME/SHAP, model transparency in safety-critical systems
  • Anomaly Detection — Physiological signal analysis, unsupervised outlier detection, dataset construction
  • Federated & Edge Learning — Ultra-lightweight models, privacy-preserving distributed training, TinyML
  • Precision Agriculture & Medical Imaging — Plant disease recognition, MRI synthesis, multimodal healthcare analytics
